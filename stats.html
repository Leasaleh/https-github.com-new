Internet usage in Lebanon

In early 2025, 4.68 million cellular mobile connections were activated in Lebanon which is equivalent to approximately 80.4% of the Lebanese population.[13] Lebanon has faced great challenges regarding the infrastructure and economic problems which has infact affected the internet quality in the country. However, Lebanon rely greatly on the Internet for education, buisness and communication services.

Speed of the Internet

In the 1990s the only way to access the Internet was by using modems that are connected to standard telephone lines. In the early 1993, the fasted modem had a speed of 14.4 kilobits per second. In 1994, the modem was doubled for a speed of 28.8k. Then, the 33.6k modem was introduced in 1996 followed by a 56k modem in 1998. In March 2000, ADSL Home Broadband were introduced, which had a speed of 512k per second, hence nine times faster than the 56k modem, however it was rather expensive. In August 2003, a 1Mb/s connection speed was launched by BT. In early 2005, the speed doubled to 2Mb which was then more optamize in March 2006 to attain 8Mb/s. In 2011 a 100Mb/s fibre broadband was offered for the first time in Milton Keynes in 2011. A 500Mb/s fibre service began rise in 2015.[14] In 2024, UAE was the country in the middle east with the fastest internet speed of 441.89 Mbps, followed by Qatar with a speed of 358.27 Mbps and Kuwait at 263.59 Mbps and then Egypt with a speed of 24.17 Mbps.[15]
