How was the Web Born

The World Wide Web was created in 1989 by a British scientist named Tim Berners-Lee working at the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN). The main intention was to create a way for the scientists to share information around the world.[1] In 1989 and 1990 Tim Berners-Lee wrote his first and second proposal for the World Wide Web.

Growth of the Web

After its conception the web evolved. With the rise of the web, it transformed in a platform for communication, commerce and entertainment around the world. The Web has become more accessible to the public. The rise of e-commerce companies in the late 1990s and 2000s allowed the web to rise in a new marketplace.[2]

Trends of the Web

Users interact differently with web each year. The behavior of these users towards the web is the key to the new trends that change each year.[3]

The Future

Looking forward, the browsers will keep getting better. The need of the consumers will also avolve over time so many important changes need to be done. Browsers are expected to be faster, more protected, to give us more features.[4] Greater security is also demanded for the future of the Web as the users have a major focus on making it hard for the hackers to access their personal informations.
